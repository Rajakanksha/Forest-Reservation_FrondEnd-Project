RESERVATION OF FOREST

A reserved forest (also called reserve forest) and protected forest in India are terms denoting forests accorded a certain degree of protection. The term was first introduced in the Indian Forest Act, 1927 in British Raj, to refer to certain forests granted protection under the British crown in British India, but not associated suzerainties. After Indian independence, the Government of India retained the status of the existing reserved and protected forests, as well as incorporating new reserved and protected forests. Many forests which came under the jurisdiction of the Government of India during the political integration of India were initially granted such protection. The first Reserve Forest Of India was Satpura National Park.


Name: Deer
Scientific Name: Cervidae
Food: Grasses, sedges, the leaves and shoots of trees and other woody plants
Habitat: They live in wetlands, deciduous forests, grasslands, rain forests, arid scrublands and mountains.

Name: Elephant
Scientific Name: Loxodonta
Food: Elephants eat parts of the plant, and spit out the leaves and seeds, which help to propagate the plant
Habitat: They are found most often in savannas, grasslands, and forests.

Name: Monkey
Scientific Name: Cercopithecidae
Food: Monkeys do eat fruits, but they wouldn't encounter bananas like we could get at the grocery store in the forest.
Habitat: They tend to live in forest regions where they have lots of trees around them.

Name: Giraffe
Scientific Name: Giraffa
Food: Giraffe are predominantly browsers and mainly eat leaves and buds on trees and shrubs.
Habitat: They live in wetlands, deciduous forests, grasslands, rain forests, arid scrublands and mountains.

Name: Lion
Scientific Name: Panthera leo
Food: Lions usually hunt and eat medium-sized to large hoofed animals like wildebeests, zebras, and antelopes.
Habitat: Prime habitat for lions is open woodlands, thick grassland, and brush habitat where there is enough cover for hunting and denning.

Name: Tiger
Scientific Name: Panthera tigris
Food: Tigers eat a variety of prey ranging in size from termites to elephant calves.
Habitat: TTigers are found in amazingly diverse habitats: rain forests, grasslands, savannas and even mangrove swamps.

Name: Cheetah
Scientific Name: Acinonyx jubatus
Food: Gazelles (especially Gazella thompsonii), impalas (Aepyceros melampus), other small- to medium-sized ungulates.
Habitat: Cheetahs are tolerant of a wide range of habitats including shrublands, grasslands, savannahs, and temperate to hot deserts.

Name: Bear
Scientific Name: Ursidae
Food: The bear is omnivorous. Its diet consists of berries, grain, fish, insects, birds and mammals
Habitat: Brown bears can be found in many habitats, from the fringes of deserts to high mountain forests and ice fields.
